About this Dataset

Dataset Description :

  • Sector: The various GE Vernova energy sectors (e.g., Gas Power, Wind Power, Grid Solutions, Solar & Storage).
  • Product/Service: Specific products or services in each sector.
  • Region: Geographic regions where these products/services are applied.
  • Problem_Type: Machine learning problems related to each sector (e.g., Classification, Regression).
  • Approach: The ML approaches (e.g., Random Forest, SVM).
  • Performance_Metrics: Metrics used to evaluate the models (e.g., Accuracy, RMSE).
  • Sector_Rating: A rating from 1 to 5, indicating the sectors performance.
  • Revenue_Impact: The estimated revenue impact of the product/service in millions (numeric).
  • Operational_Efficiency: The operational efficiency of the system in percentage terms (numeric).
  • Market_Share: The market share of the product/service in its sector (percentage).
  • Energy_Produced: The amount of energy produced (in gigawatt-hours or GWh) for each product (numeric).
  • Maintenance_Cost: The annual maintenance cost (in millions) for each product/service (numeric).
  • Failure_Rate: The failure rate of the system or product over time (percentage).
  • Carbon_Footprint: Carbon emissions (in tons) associated with each product/service (numeric).
  • Customer_Satisfaction_Score: Satisfaction score from customers (on a scale of 1 to 100).
  • Innovation_Index: A score representing the level of innovation in the product/service (1 to 10).
  • Digitalization_Level: The degree of digital integration (percentage) in the system (numeric).
  • Asset_Lifetime: The estimated operational lifetime of the product (in years).
  • Downtime_Hours: The number of hours the equipment was non-operational (downtime) annually.
  • Upgrade_Frequency: How frequently (in years) the product requires upgrades or maintenance.
  • Energy_Efficiency_Rating: Rating for energy efficiency (on a scale of 1 to 10).
  • Supply_Chain_Risk: The risk level associated with supply chain disruptions (percentage).
  • Cybersecurity_Score: A score reflecting the system's cybersecurity resilience (on a scale of 1 to 100).
  • R&D_Investment: The amount invested in research and development (in millions).
  • Employee_Productivity_Index: A score reflecting employee productivity in managing and operating the equipment (1 to 100).
  • AI_Integration_Level: The percentage of AI-powered automation and decision-making integrated into the system.
  • Market_Growth_Potential: The potential for growth in the specific market for this product (percentage).
  • CO2_Reduction_Target: Target reduction in CO2 emissions (percentage).
  • Government_Regulation_Score: A score representing the impact of government regulations (on a scale of 1 to 100).

# Optuna objective function to optimize different models
def objective(trial):
    # Choose the model type
    model_type = trial.suggest_categorical("model", ["ridge", "decision_tree", "random_forest", "gradient_boosting", "xgboost", "mlp"])

    if model_type == "ridge":
        # Ridge Regression
        alpha = trial.suggest_float("alpha", 1e-3, 10.0, log=True)
        model = Ridge(alpha=alpha)

    elif model_type == "decision_tree":
        # Decision Tree Regressor
        max_depth = trial.suggest_int("max_depth", 2, 32)
        min_samples_split = trial.suggest_int("min_samples_split", 2, 20)
        model = DecisionTreeRegressor(max_depth=max_depth, min_samples_split=min_samples_split)

    elif model_type == "random_forest":
        # Random Forest Regressor
        n_estimators = trial.suggest_int("n_estimators", 100, 1000)
        max_depth = trial.suggest_int("max_depth", 2, 32)
        model = RandomForestRegressor(n_estimators=n_estimators, max_depth=max_depth)

    elif model_type == "gradient_boosting":
        # Gradient Boosting Regressor
        learning_rate = trial.suggest_float("learning_rate", 1e-3, 0.1, log=True)
        n_estimators = trial.suggest_int("n_estimators", 100, 1000)
        max_depth = trial.suggest_int("max_depth", 2, 32)
        model = GradientBoostingRegressor(learning_rate=learning_rate, n_estimators=n_estimators, max_depth=max_depth)

    elif model_type == "xgboost":
        # XGBoost Regressor
        learning_rate = trial.suggest_float("learning_rate", 1e-3, 0.1, log=True)
        n_estimators = trial.suggest_int("n_estimators", 100, 1000)
        max_depth = trial.suggest_int("max_depth", 2, 32)
        model = XGBRegressor(learning_rate=learning_rate, n_estimators=n_estimators, max_depth=max_depth)

    elif model_type == "mlp":
        # Neural Network (MLP)
        hidden_layer_sizes = trial.suggest_categorical("hidden_layer_sizes", [(50,50), (100,100), (50,100)])
        activation = trial.suggest_categorical("activation", ["relu", "tanh"])
        learning_rate_init = trial.suggest_float("learning_rate_init", 1e-4, 1e-2, log=True)
        model = MLPRegressor(hidden_layer_sizes=hidden_layer_sizes, activation=activation, learning_rate_init=learning_rate_init)

    # Cross-validation scoring
    score = cross_val_score(model, X_train_scaled, y_train, cv=3, scoring='neg_mean_squared_error').mean()

    return -score
